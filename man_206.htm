Course Description

 

The course content includes modelling linear programs, review of matrix algebra and geometry, duality and optimality conditions in LP, primal algorithm for the transportation problem, Hungarian method, the simplex method for general LP, duality theory and sensitivity analysis, algorithms for multi-objective models, modelling integer and combinatorial programs, the branch and bound method.

 

Text and Other Readings

 

The textbook for the course is Operations Research: Applications and Algorithms by Wayne L. Winston (Duxbury Press). Chapters from this book have been assigned as background reading with the material being covered.

 

Some other text books on the subject that may be of interest are as follows:

 

1.    Operations Research: An Introduction by Hamdy A. Taha (Prentice Hall)

2.    Introduction to Operations Research by F. S. Hillier and G. J. Lieberman (McGrawHill)

3.    Optimization in Operations Research by Ronald L. Rardin (Prentice Hall)

4.    Operations Research Models and Methods by Paul A. Jensen and Jonathan F. Bard (Wiley)

 

Other required readings will be provided in class when necessary.
