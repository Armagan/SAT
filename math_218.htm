Course Objectives

 

This course aims to provide basic and some further concepts of probability and random processes including the axioms of probability, Bayes' theorem, random variables and sums of random variables, law of large numbers, the central limit theorem and its applications, confidence intervals, discrete and continuous-time random processes, elementary introduction to queueing theory.

 

Text and Other Readings

 

The textbook for the course is Probability and Stochastic  Processes: A Friendly Introduction for Electrical and Computer Engineers, Roy D. Yates and David J. Goodman. Chapters from this book have been assigned as background reading with the material being covered.

 

Some other text books on the subject that may be of interest are as follows:

 

1.    Introduction to Probability Models by Sheldon Ross (Academic Press)

2.    Probability, Random Variables and Stochastic Processes by A. Papoulis and S. U. Pillai (McGrawHill)

 

Other required readings will be provided in class when necessary.
